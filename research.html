Algebra

  1. On graded trivial extensions with homogeneous derivations
    Submitted
    Let $G$ be an arbitrary group, $R = \bigoplus_{\tau \in G} R_\tau$ a $G$-graded ring, and $M$ a graded $R$-bimodule. Every homogeneous derivation on the graded trivial extension $R \ltimes M$ admits a canonical decomposition into four component maps. The notion of a $\delta$-gr-simple ring is introduced, and a graded analogue of Posner's theorem is established in prime characteristic.
  2. On graded differential polynomial rings
    Submitted
    Let $R = \bigoplus_{\tau \in G} R_\tau$ be a $G$-graded ring and $\delta$ a degree-preserving derivation. We study $R[t;\delta]$ and establish its universal property in the category of graded rings. A complete characterization of gr-simplicity is obtained: $R[t;\delta]$ is gr-simple if and only if $R$ is $\delta$-gr-simple and $Z(R[t;\delta])$ is a graded field.
  3. Generalized homogeneous derivations on graded rings
    Siberian Mathematical Journal — To appear
    We introduce a notion of generalized homogeneous derivations on graded rings. Several significant results for prime rings are extended to gr-prime rings, and we characterize conditions under which gr-semiprime rings contain nontrivial central graded ideals.
  4. On graded rings with homogeneous derivations
    Revista de la Unión Matemática Argentina — Published   [DOI]
    We establish results related to homogeneous derivations. We extend Posner's and Herstein's theorems from prime rings to gr-prime and gr-semiprime rings.
