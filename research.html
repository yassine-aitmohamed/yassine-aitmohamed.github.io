  1. On graded trivial extensions with homogeneous derivations
    Submitted
    Let $G$ be an arbitrary group, $R = \bigoplus_{\tau \in G} R_\tau$ a $G$-graded ring, and $M$ a graded $R$-bimodule. Every homogeneous derivation on the graded trivial extension $R \ltimes M$ admits a canonical decomposition into four component maps, extending the ungraded result of Mokhtari--Moafian--Vishki to the $G$-graded setting. The notion of a $\delta$-gr-simple ring is introduced, and a graded analogue of Posner's theorem is established in prime characteristic. For diagonal derivations $\delta = (d, D)$, the $\delta$-invariant gr-ideals of $R \ltimes M$ are characterized via the conditions $d(\mathfrak{a}) \subseteq \mathfrak{a}$ and $D(N) \subseteq N$, and $R \ltimes M$ is shown to be $\delta$-gr-simple relative to $0 \ltimes M$ if and only if $R$ is $d$-gr-simple and $M$ is $D$-gr-simple as a graded $R$-module.
  2. On graded differential polynomial rings
    Submitted
    Let $R = \bigoplus_{\tau \in G} R_\tau$ be a $G$-graded ring, where $G$ is an arbitrary group, and let $\delta$ be a degree-preserving derivation on $R$. We introduce the graded differential polynomial ring $R[t; \delta]$ and present several main results. We formulate a universal property of $R[t; \delta]$ in the category of graded rings and derive isomorphisms under conjugate derivations, inner shifts, and matrix ring extensions. When $R$ is gr-simple of characteristic zero and $\delta$ is dp-outer, we show that $R[t; \delta]$ is gr-simple, providing a graded analogue of Amitsur’s classical theorem. We obtain a complete characterization of gr-simplicity: $R[t; \delta]$ is gr-simple if and only if $R$ is $\delta$-gr-simple and the center $Z(R[t; \delta])$ is a graded field. This equivalence holds in arbitrary characteristic. Finally, we prove that the gr-Noetherian property, both on the left and on the right, is preserved under the construction of $R[t; \delta]$; in particular, we show that the graded polynomial ring $R[t]$ inherits the gr-Noetherian property from $R$.
  3. Generalized homogeneous derivations on graded rings
    Siberian Mathematical Journal
    To appear
    We introduce a notion of generalized homogeneous derivations on graded rings as a natural extension of the homogeneous derivations defined by Kanunnikov. We then define gr-generalized derivations, which preserve the degrees of homogeneous components. Several significant results originally established for prime rings are extended to the setting of gr-prime rings, and we characterize conditions under which gr-semiprime rings contain nontrivial central graded ideals.
  4. On graded rings with homogeneous derivations
    Revista de la Unión Matemática Argentina
    Published
    We establish results related to homogeneous derivations, a concept introduced by Kanunnikov (2018). First, we prove the existence of a non-trivial family of derivations that are not homogeneous on graded rings. Furthermore, based on homogeneous derivations, we extend certain existing significant results in the context of prime (resp. semiprime) rings to gr-prime (resp. gr-semiprime) rings, such as Posner's and Herstein's theorems.
